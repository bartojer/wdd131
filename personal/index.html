Avocado pit and skin dye: When I first began my natural dye journey, discovering very unassuming kitchen scraps as tannin-rich dyes took me by surprise. All of these years and I never knew that avocado pits and skins, for example, deliver color-fast hues of pink. Yes, you read that right. Pink!

Goldenrod, with its vibrant yellow blooms, is a natural dye that offers fabric makers a unique, eco-friendly way to achieve warm, sunny hues. The process typically involves simmering dried goldenrod to release its rich pigments, then using a mordant—commonly alum—to help the dye bond securely to the fibers. The result is a soft, luminous color that not only adds a vintage charm to textiles but also embraces sustainable, botanical dyeing practices.

Goldenrod and marigolds work well in eco printing, where their natural dyes are transferred onto fabric using a hammer. Arrange the flowers on pre-treated fabric, then gently press with a hammer to release their pigments and create unique patterns. This sustainable method offers a hands-on way to capture nature's vibrant hues.

Hi, my name is Lou. I'm an eco-conscious artisan based in the heart of the American West, drawing inspiration from the rugged beauty of the Idaho and Utah region. Passionate about sustainable practices, I specialize in natural dyeing and eco printing techniques that harness the vibrant hues of local flora. My work reflects a deep respect for traditional craftsmanship and a commitment to creating one-of-a-kind, environmentally friendly textiles that celebrate the unique character of my region.
